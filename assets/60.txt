    POTATO RICE BREAD
Preparation Time: 10 minutes RISING TIME: 1 HOUR Cooking Time: 45 MINUTES
 This bread uses potato flour and brown rice flour – no wheat flour –
  so it is great for anyone with wheat allergies.
Note: This recipe is originally from the revised edition of Antonietta Terrigno’s previous
 book entitled Italian Cooking, Classic Recipes, printed in May, 2005.


INGREDIENTS
    INSTRUCTIONS
1/3 cup (75 mL) pure potato flour  -
    
3 cups (750 mL) pure brown rice flour
1/4 cup (60 mL) sugar   
11/2 tsp (7 mL) salt   -
2/3 cup (150 mL) dry milk   
    -
1 tbsp (15 mL) instant dry yeast
    
2 large eggs
1/4 cup (60 mL) shortening, melted  -
13/4 cups (425 mL) warm water  
    
    
    
    -
    -


INSTRUCTIONS
 In a large bowl, combine the dry
 ingredients, including the yeast.
 Set aside.
 Whip the eggs until frothy. Add the
 shortening and warm water.
 Thoroughly mix the dry ingredients 
 into the liquid mixture.
 Divide the dough in 2 and place into 2, 
 4" x 8" (10 x 20 cm) greased loaf pans.
 Let rise in a warm oven, 200°F (93°C),
 for 1 hour, or until the bread reaches 
 the top of the pan.
 Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 45 minutes.
 Makes 2 loaves.
